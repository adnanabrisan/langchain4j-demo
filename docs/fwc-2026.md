The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the biggest World Cup yet: 48 teams, 104 matches, and a three-country host setup across the United States, Canada, and Mexico. It runs from 11 June 2026 to 19 July 2026, with the opener at Mexico City’s Estadio Azteca and the final at MetLife Stadium in New York/New Jersey. ## Overview

This edition is historic because it is the first World Cup staged across three host nations and the first with 48 teams instead of 32. FIFA’s official statistics page says it is tracking team and player performance, goals, assists, and more throughout the tournament. [fifa](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/statistics)

## Dates and format

The tournament opened on 11 June 2026 and will conclude on 19 July 2026. It features 104 matches, which is a major expansion from previous editions and reflects the larger 48-team format. [news](https://www.news.ro/sport/lista-completa-a-tarilor-calificate-pana-acum-la-cupa-mondiala-din-2026-1922403116332025111822239504)

## Host countries

The host countries are the United States, Canada, and Mexico. That arrangement makes this the first men’s World Cup to be played simultaneously in three countries, and the sites are spread across major cities in all three nations. [euronews](https://www.euronews.ro/articole/echipele-tarile-calificate-la-cupa-mondiala-2026)

## Stadiums and venues

The opening match is scheduled for Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, and the final is set for MetLife Stadium in the New York/New Jersey area. A venue list published by FIFA World Cup news sources includes major stadiums across the host cities, showing how the tournament is distributed across North America. ## Teams and countries

As of the latest updated qualification lists, the field includes the three host nations plus qualified teams from Europe, Africa, Asia, Oceania, South America, and North and Central America.
