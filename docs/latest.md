## FIFA World Cup 2026: latest picture

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first men’s World Cup with **48 teams**, **104 matches**, and **three host countries**: the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The tournament began on **11 June 2026** and is scheduled to end on **19 July 2026**, with the opening match in Mexico City and the final at MetLife Stadium in the New York/New Jersey area. ## Stadiums and venue context

The tournament is being played across **16 cities** in North America, with major venues spread through the U.S., Canada, and Mexico. FIFA’s updated schedule confirmed the opening at **Estadio Azteca** in Mexico City and the final at **MetLife Stadium** in New York/New Jersey, while other key venues include stadiums in Atlanta, Dallas, Houston, Guadalajara, Toronto, and Los Angeles. [mlssoccer](https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-schedule-all-games-full-list-of-matchups)

That venue spread is important because travel, climate, and local atmosphere all influence the tournament experience. For teams, it means adapting to different conditions across a continent-sized host setup. For fans, it means the World Cup feels more like a North American festival than a single-city event.
